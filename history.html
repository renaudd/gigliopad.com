The Painter’s Pad

Gigliopad isn’t just a rental; it’s a slice of Roman creative history. This building was the longtime home and studio of Ramón Gaya, the legendary Spanish painter. You can still feel that "artist-in-residence" energy in the high ceilings and the way the light hits the floor in the afternoon.

We’re located in Rione Regola, a neighborhood that’s managed to stay inherently cool while the rest of the city gets glossy. It’s the kind of place where you’ll find a 400-year-old fountain next to a modern art gallery.

"Fleur-de-lis means lily flower in French; Giglio means lily in Italian. Vicolo del Giglio (Lily Lane) derives its name from the crest of the House Farnese—a field of fleur-de-lis—whose historic palace stands just steps away in Piazza Farnese."

House Farnese Vibes

Being next door to the Piazza Farnese means we’re basically neighbors with royalty. The House Farnese crest—that iconic field of lilies—gave our street its name. It’s a nice reminder that while we’re living in a modern pad, we’re surrounded by some of the most powerful history in Europe.
