Master Booking Checklist

Rome rewards the prepared. Here is the temporal map for your reservations.

Advance Bookings

2 Months Out
Galleria Borghese: Strictly mandatory. Tickets disappear instantly.
Armando al Pantheon: Tiny and historic. Book as soon as you have your dates.
3 Weeks Out
Seu Pizza Illuminati: The best modern dough in the city.
Felice a Testaccio: A Cacio e Pepe institution.
2 Weeks Out
Vatican Museums: Aim for the 8:00 AM slot.
The Colosseum: Look for the "Full Experience" tickets.

Flight & Rail Mastery

Google Flights: Use the "Track Prices" feature for FCO. Tuesday and Wednesday flights are typically 20% cheaper than weekends.

Eurail Pass: If you are planning 3+ train trips across Italy (e.g., Florence, Venice, Naples), a Eurail pass is worth the flexibility. If just staying in Rome, skip it and use regional/Frecciarossa tickets.

The "Sold Out" Backdoor

If the official museum sites are sold out, look for Guided Tours. Companies like Walks of Italy or The Roman Guy often have reserved blocks of tickets. Even a tour in a language you don't speak (like Spanish) is a valid way to get through the door.
