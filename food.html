Insider Eats

Contemporary Neapolitan Pizza
Piccolo Buco

If you have come for the canotto, this might be your favorite experience in all of Italy.

If you show up before they open, you might have little-to-no wait. Otherwise count yourself lucky if you only have to wait 50 minutes. If you're in a group, and can take turns waiting in line, this is a good opportunity to souvenir shop and throw a coin in the Trevi.

If you want comparable pizza and would rather plan a month ahead than stand an hour in line, grab a reservation at Seu Pizza Illuminati (Via Angelo Bargoni).

Roman Pizza
Emma

Thin-crust, traditional Roman pizza with high-end ingredients. It’s busy, it’s loud, and it’s one of the best spots in the center for a late dinner with friends. Just a 5-minute walk from the pad. For a more casual slice, try Antico Forno Roscioli (Via dei Chiavari).

The Caffeine Hub
Barnum Roma

Our go-to for morning coffee or a productive brunch. It’s got that creative shop energy and the best avocado toast in Regola. Perfect for meeting other travelers or getting some work done.

Neighborhood Hidden Gems
Ruma Bottega

A modern masterclass in agricultural cuisine. Their cheese boards and focus on Maremma-sourced ingredients provide a fresh, contemporary contrast to the heavy Roman classics. Located just around the corner at Via di Tor Argentina.

Cocktail Culture
The Jerry Thomas Project

Rome’s most famous speakeasy. You’ll need a password (and a reservation), but the drinks are world-class. It’s dark, moody, and exactly the kind of place we love.
